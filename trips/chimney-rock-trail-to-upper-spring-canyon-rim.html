Chimney Rock trail to Upper Spring Canyon rim

2026-04-12 - Chimney Rock trail to Upper Spring Canyon rim

At the visitor center, I asked a ranger for nice off trail peak hikes north of the Fremont River but he said he didn't know much but would get someone who did. He turned around and went through a door and a few moments later an older guy popped in, I believe a volunteer. He suggested this route and drew a rough diagram, graciously describing the route, where to turn, and other advice, ending by saying it was a dawn to dusk type hike. It was to the park benchmark (highest peak in the park) called Capitol. I was sold or so i thought! The route up from upper spring canyon has a lot of small boulders and a couple spots needing scrambling moves. It was a not very fun climb up to the rim where the slope finally abated a bit but the annoyance was replaced by tons of cryptobiotic soil. It was pretty much everywhere and difficult to avoid stepping on. I wasted a lot of time carefully avoiding and walking around the soil looking for slick rock to walk on. I finally got near a minor outcropping with a small bridge and got a good lay of the land from this point. There didn't seem to be many interesting landmarks within the flat rim/basin that I was in. I gave up on trying to make it over to Capitol Benchmark which was probably another 8 miles roundtrip, so headed east but it was slow going with much crypto soil. After trying a few different directions I turned around and headed down. It was a good scouting trip and useful to know a route up though I'm not sure if there are many interesting routes once atop the rim, and if there are, they will be slow going navigating around the soil and terrain.
