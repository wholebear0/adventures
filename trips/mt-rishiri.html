Mt Rishiri

2026-07-03 - Mt Rishiri

Rishiri island is shaped like a big circle with one mountain in the very middle, Mt Rishiri, which at 5600ft towers over the ocean below. It is one of Japan's 100 famous mountains and part of the national park. This was the main reason I went up so far in Hokkaido and I really wanted to hike it so I waited out two days of rain camping near Wakkanai. On the first clear day I took the earliest ferry to the island. There are two small towns on the island and the ferry lands at one of them. From that point there was a 30 minute walk to a campground next to a nice onsen facility. The campground was nice with amenities like running water, a food truck, community power outlet, but overly crowded which was a sign of things to come. I rented a bicycle from the Rishiri Inn hostel about a 40 minute walk from the campground and spent the rest of the day having fun cycling around the paved greenway trail on the island. There was a small parade of sorts at one point with high school age kids dressed up and carrying some sort of float, not sure what it was. The next day I woke early and started hiking around 5:30 am. There weren't many people and the first 2/3 of the hike went mostly through generic green forest with limited to no views of anything but dirt looking down and green vegetation on both sides. The hike went pretty much straight up the mountain in a line. Eventually, there was a small flat rest area and hut 2/3 of the way up. The view out over the ocean below was great but the trail got extremely crowded waiting for people to pass, waiting behind people, 20 person hiking tour groups, etc. Views from the summit were good as well though not 360 degrees because access to the south summit was closed. At the summit next to the shrine a man was boiling water on a portable stove for his cup of noodles. Though some snow exists in the gulleys, no snow was encountered during the hike. The mountain could probbaly be climbed without snow several weeks earlier in the year I imagine. In the far distance you could see Russia and a bit closer was Rebun island where I went the following day. In the opposite direction was Hokkaido and Wakkanai/
