Toiyabe Crest Trail and Twin River Loop

7/4/2025 - Toiyabe Crest Trail and Twin River Loop

After last weekend hiking among crowds on Mt Humphrey in Flagstaff, I wanted a quiet place to backpack for the July 4th weekend. The Arc Dome Wilderness is the largest wilderness area in Nevada and quite remote, several hours from Las Vegas and a 5 hr drive from St George. I arrived in the late afternoon and car camped the first night. There was a big lightning storm and downpour that night but the weather the next morning was fantastic. The temperatures were in the 60s to 70s with a nice breeze. Most of the 31 mile trail followed along a stream or river. Unfortunately, two miles past the trailhead the trail started to be very overgrown which continued for the entirety of the hike. There were numerous stream crossings to wade through, mile after mile of knee to chest high brush to push past, and many times where the trail just went off straight into a dense forest of trees and overgrown branches. It was a slow going hike but there was no one else out there aside from two groups near the start and end. Plus it was nice hiking nearly every step near flowing water, whether it was a stream or spring flowing down a mountain, with grass covered hills and ridges in the background. Eventually the route led up a canyon and crossed a table land, over 11,000’ in elevation where the views were really nice. Then the route went down and to the most difficult part of the hike where the trail entirely disappeared. There were about 6 miles of bushwhacking through thick desert bushes and pushing aside tree branches. At one point the canyon narrowed and the mountains seemed to rise straight out of the river, quite a scenic view reminding me of the Tetons. It was a nice hike but unfortunate that most of the trail was so overgrown or abandoned.

Nice views but no trail and a hard time navigating. I knew I had to descend to the low point towards the middle left. I ultimately cut over the hills and found myself in dense, chest deep, thick brush.

Further down the canyon going through the trees was next to impossible so I bypassed it by hiking along a ridge to the left.

There were some curves in the canyon which I think was the crux of the hike as it required navigating over loose, steep rock. It took about 2 hours to go 1 mile. A decent trail would take maybe 40 minutes.

The mountains rose straight up out of the river and reminded me of the Tetons in Wyoming.
