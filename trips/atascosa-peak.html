Atascosa Peak

2026-05-02 - Atascosa Peak

Atascosa Peak is a somewhat obscure and remote peak, west of Nogales and about 5 miles north of the Mexico border. Turning off I-19, the road is paved to a small lake, Pena Blanca, but is followed by about 5 miles of very washboarded roads to the trailhead. The elevation of the surrounding area is in the high 4000s so there are some trees and greenery. There was no one else at the trailhead when I was there and I saw no one the entire time which made for a very peaceful hike. This was a really nice hike with views nearly the entire time, starting from the trailhead. The hike makes a quick ascent to the Atascosa Lookout mountain which offers a view of the actual Atascosa Mountain .75 miles north. The way from the lookout to Atascosa Mountain is an unofficial social trail but it is in very good condition with minimal to no bush whacking, and follows along a ridge with nice views to the east and west. Below Atascosa Mountain is a short scramble which should take no more than 5 minutes. The views on top were pretty good though not any better than the atop the lookout. The last entry in the summit register was in February, about 3 months ago. The hike started out with cloudless skies but the moderate wind had brought in numerous clouds very quickly. The temperature was really nice and after a short rest I turned around and headed back to the car, wishing the hike was longer than it was. To the southwest is Sycamore Canyon which looked really nice from where I was so I hope to return to the area another time. Perhaps in November to see fall colors along the creek.
