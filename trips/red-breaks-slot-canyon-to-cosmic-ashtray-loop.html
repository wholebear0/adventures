Adventures - Red Breaks Slot Canyon to Cosmic Ashtray Loop

5/25/2025 - Red Breaks Slot Canyon to Cosmic Ashtray Loop

An insanely fun hike and one of my top 10 favorites to date. The road to the trailhead was rough and extremely wash boarded but doable in an SUV. I began the hike around 7:30 am and hiked up through a wash which eventually narrowed after 2 miles or so. In the next mile the slots started and there were a handful of chokestones to navigate over by stemming or chimneying up and over- done by pushing the feet against one side of the wall and pushing the back against the wall while walking oneself up. The boulder obstacles were incredibly fun and I soon exited the slot and got to the junction with an impassable hundred foot pour off. After backtracking a bit there was a route up a steep ledge system. Looking back, it was a little dicey as I had to climb almost straight up using cracks as handholds. After bypassing the pour off the wide canyon narrowed into a thin slot. This is where the fun started. The slot got so narrow I couldn't take one step with my foot flat on the ground. Sometimes it would open up a few feet and there would be more boulder obstacles, probably at least 20 to navigate past, pretty much all of them requiring me to either stem or chimney over, pull myself up, or devise some other means of negotiating them. At one point there were boulders or chockstones every 20-30 feet, then it would narrow. It was a gauntlet for sure and I was getting a gym workout. One of the last obstacles was a 15-20 foot boulder with a flat side and no handholds, with a thin 1 foot wide gulley on the side. It took a lot of effort to climb up through that one. Then there was a 10 foot climb up through a small hole between rocks. I inched upwards holding onto rocks for dear life, not looking down, with each part of my body, feet, thighs, back, and hands, all applying force in different direction onto rock. To top the gauntlet off was a stretch of slot about 50 feet long that I could barely squeeze through. Being so narrow it was quite dark and at one point I got plum stuck, and had to remove my pack and camera  and even poles. I developed a strategy on the spot of inching forward. I removed my pack, camera, and hiking poles, then lifted up the pack, set it down a half foot further, picked up the poles and camera and set it down after the pack, then would take a right step forward, take a left foot forward, and then rinse and repeat. It was insanely fun but I started to ponder when this party would end. Eventually it did and I found myself shaking, maybe from adrenaline, tired muscles, or low blood sugar, or quite probably, all three. It was a fun 4 miles of will testing, non-stop action. Right as I exited the slot there was a rattlesnake on the ground. There simply was nowhere for me to go so I waited a few minutes until it slid under a rock. As it did I jumped past as it rattled its tail. After the slot ended the hike was about half over with the Cosmic Ashtray ahead a few miles. I hiked cross country through slick rock and ravines, then climbed the volcano to where the Cosmic Ashtray is. I hiked around and gazed at the sky with the deep blue rain clouds in the distance that had yet to drop any rain. At last I found the cosmic ashtray bowl which was really cool and other worldly. Afterwards I hiked down towards a 4x4 road and passed one group, the only I had seen today. There was about 5 miles of uneventful road walking back to the car with no cars passing. Once in a while a strong gust would blow dust in my eyes and there were few light raindrops but the hike back went quick, leaving time to reminisce about how awesome the past few hours had been.
