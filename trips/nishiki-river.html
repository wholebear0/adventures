Nishiki River

2026-06-10 - Nishiki River

About an hour and half train ride from Hiroshima near the city of Iwakuni is the Nishiki river. To scout it out I had looked on Google maps and found that the river appeared to be floatable and free of dams and other obstructions. Other than that I had no idea what it would be like or what I would be getting into. After stumbling around trying to find the right train in the big Hiroshima station, I rode for an hour then transferred to a small one car train at Iwakuni. A couple got on who were also packrafting which provided some assurance. They mentioned the river was too shallow a couple weeks ago but that now was good and that it had a good flow. They were very friendly. I got off at the Mukuni station and after some searching for a way down the bridge to the riverbank, found some steps down where I put in. The flow was decent mostly but slow like a lake in a few spots. The water was shallow and very clear, more clear than the Shimanto and Niyodo rivers. The only obstacles during the trip were one or two shallow water portages. I scraped the raft against rocks once or twice so I imagine other times of the year outside of the rainy season would be too shallow. Much bamboo padded the river banks and once in a while there were dead stalks poking up from the water which had to be avoided. I think I was lucky with timing. The water was just high enough so a sunny day in the rainy season might be one of the better times to float. The couple I spoke with had gotten off the train a couple miles earlier but we ran into each other by the bridge take out, where they took a photo and later emailed it to me. Afterwards, I packed the raft up, got annoyed at having to pay a "toll" to walk across the bridge, then hiked through a really cool historic village area up to a castle overlooking Iwakuni and the Nishiki river.
