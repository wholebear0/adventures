Adventures - Hackberry, Stone Donkey, Hogeye, and Paria Canyons

4/25 /2 5 - 4/26/25 - Hackberry, Stone Donkey, Hogeye, and Paria Canyons

A 28 mile overnight backpacking trip loop through several canyons. It was a quiet, peaceful, and very enjoyable walk. I didn't see a single person the whole time. It started with an 8 mile hike through Lower Hackberry canyon through creek and narrows lined with bright green cottonwood trees. At times the canyon suddenly opened up to fields and bushes and sun and narrowed just as quickly. After that I made an exit to the left and entered Stone Donkey canyon for a a couple mile hike to the slot canyon. The slot was awesome though I could only make it through 3/4 of it. Supposedly the best part is at the end where there is a chamber of sorts but I couldnt get all the way back to it. I had to backtrack a mile and exit the canyon, then walk around to the beginning of the slot but from above, then walked cross country through sand hills dotted with Juniper and cow pies to the start of Hogeye Canyon. Just before the canyon I tried walking down a section of slickrock and wiped out and got a few scratches. I intended to camp somewhere in Hoegeye Canyon, before the Paria river, but there were few if any flat sections to throw down the tent. It was late, around 7:30 pm, so I picked up the pace and finished the canyon where I then forded the Paria River getting my feet wet, then set up camp. The next morning I walked back up Hogeye Canyon a little ways to collect water to drink. The Paria is a long river and dotted with ranches and livestock so the water shouldnt be consumed. The remaining 10 miles was a nice flat walk alongside the river.

The one resource I had for the planning the hike was a book written in the early 80s. Problem is things can change in 40 years. Supposedly th is was called an "excellent spring" but I spent a half hour searching in the area for it and came up empty. After a few minutes of careful consideration on whether to collect the water to drank, I ultimately backtracked and found water coming out of the rock, probably from a spring.

Along the overhang I did find water coming out of the rock, which I collected with difficulty as it was only a trickle. I drank about a half liter and ate lunch of salmon and half a poptart. I was thirsty. The water tasted a little like lemon and I dont know if it was the food or what, but had a few burps afterwards.
