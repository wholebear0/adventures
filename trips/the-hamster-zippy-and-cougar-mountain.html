The Hamster, Zippy, and Cougar Mountain

3/30 /25 - The Hamster, Zippy, and Cougar Mountain

This hike had been on my to-do list for over 3 years and I finally was able to check it out today. The entirety of the hike was pure back country, that is, no established trails. I gathered some information and routes from lurking within backcountry forums like Summitpost. I decided to do an overnight backpack trip to allow enough time to check out the scenery and make a few summits that few people have done. I was a bit nervous of how Trail Canyon would play out. Reports mentioned tons of mud and steep ascents over loose rock and sand. The reports weren’t wrong. Trail canyon took about 2-3 hours and was easier coming down the second day of the backpack trip. After passing some dryfall which required steep routes around them, wading through mud and a creek, I finally made the saddle between Cougar mountain and the mesa opposite from it. I was hoping there would be a small waterfall near Terry wash and was one reason I chose to go this time of the year in late winter after snow melt. Unfortunately, it was little more than a trickle over a 600ft drop. I made my way east to a location between “The Hamster” and “The Bishropic West” and set up camp. There wasn’t much level ground so the tent wasn’t level and I would pay the price dearly at night. After setting up camp and leaving the tent, sleeping bacg and a few other items, I started the ascent of “The Hamster” and was blown away. It was 7pm and I was out of breath and dehydrated, but the late afternoon sun bounced off hoodoos, cliffs, peaks, and mesas in the 360 view that surrounded. Above and back towards Hurricane was sun but ahead towards the higher elevations were rain clouds which made for a dramatic sky. The views at the top were gorgeous and the solitude, remoteness, and quiet made it even better. After some time relaxing I headed back to camp. I got no more than 4 hours of sleep and spent most of it shivering cold, particularly bothered by my cold feet which felt like they were about to be frostbitten. To make matters worse, the tent site was not level but slanted so laying there was painful on one side of my body and prevented any sleep. It was a long night and the end of my tent collapsed, probably from me kicking the supporting hiking pole down in the cold, wee, morning hours. At 1am I was tempted to just hike back but got hold of better sense realizing that the path down trail canyon would be especially dangerous in the dark. So I waited it out and as soon as the sky lightened ever se faintly at 6:30am, I promptly packed up and left camp. On the way back I made a steep hike up the “Zippy” peak which got my heart racing. I stopped just short, maybe 20 ft, of the summit due to high winds and cold temperatures, and also because of my low water reserve. The rest of the hike back to the trailhead was uneventful. I fantasized about going to Taco Bell, ordering two potato soft tacos and a small fountain soda and drinking multiple refills, which I carried out exactly on the way back to the house. All in all, a pretty cool hike. I would like to come back in late fall and find out if the water sources are drinkable. If so I would like to spend 2-3 days, hiking some of the other peaks like The Bishropics and going further east, like to Ivins Mountain and maybe beyond.
