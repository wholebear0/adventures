Day 2 - 18.0 miles Today was another long day with nice weather, 18 miles in total. Aside from one or two miles with some mountain bikers there was hardly anybody. I hiked for a couple hours at a time without seeing anyone. The trail was very scenic as it weaved along the rim overlooking the Nevada side of Lake Tahoe. The trail then went up towards an alpine lake, Star Lake, then proceeded through the mountains and onto a ridge with a south facing view of the Sierra towards Yosemite. So far the Tahoe Rim Trail has been in excellent shape, gently graded and easy on the feet. I hiked a little longer than I planned today because I needed to find water, so I cut down a canyon towards the town of Myers and finally found a stream. However there were no flat spots to camp so I ended up going several miles further and eventually found a nice flat area to throw down the tent for the night.

The biggest Juniper tree ive ever seen. The trunk was huge- compare to the other pine trees for reference.

Day 3 - 13.9 miles Two days of seeing hardly anyone came to an end today as I entered the popular Desolation Wilderness. I descended from the mountains in the morning using the Saxon Greek route and walked through a quaint neighborhood of log cabins to Meyers and resupplied on groceries. The prices were at about 3x the prices in AZ or UT so I got very distracted and accidentally bought less food than I needed, which I would come to regret. Afterwards I walked out down the road, stopped at a cafe where I ordered a $9 bowl of oatmeal which was basically instant oats, frozen blueberries, and a spoonful of yogurt. The traffic heading east into Lake Tahoe from California was insane and bumper to bumper. I tired quickly of the commotion and took an old unused grade rode up to Lake Echo. Nearing Lake Echo were lines of cars packed back through neighborhoods and campgrounds. After a few miles hiking along the lakes, the crowd dwindled a little bit and I made my way to a small lake to Camp. Not a few minutes after I put down my gear a group of two guys appeared and wandered around the lake for a bit, presumably looking for a spot to camp. Not finding a spot, they came up and asked if they could camp right near me, a few feet away. I couldn’t believe my ears and I told them it was a little close but would be all good. They got the message and camped a few hundred feet away. Feeling sweaty and tired I took my clothes off and jumped in the lake for a swim. It was cold but refreshing and it got the sweat off.

After resupplying in town (Meyers) I headed up an abandoned old grade road towards Desolation Wilderness and Echo Lake. It was a little hot in the mid afternoon sun gaining elevation with no shade.

Echo Lake. This is the start of Desolation Wilderness and was the most crowded part of the trail. There were a lot of backpackers and hikers.

Day 4 - 20.0 miles Today was a hike through the heart of the Desolation wilderness through forest and along many lakes. The scenery was pretty good. I met someone hiking the Pacific Crest Trail who was on day 100. She started at the US/Mexico border and said she got delayed for a month or so due to injury. We hiked for a bit but she kept stopping at every stream to drink and snack so I had to say "see ya". Towards the end of the day, my blunder from the day before of not stocking up enough at the grocery store came back to bite as I realized I didnt have enough food for one more full day. Therefore I had to reroute and start back towards town. I camped next to a stream 5 or 6 miles from civilization.

Day 5 - 17.6 miles Out of food today I headed into town and passed through Sandhill State Park which was nice. The trees were so green and tall and lush and I took an hour break at small beach along the lake. Afterwards I waited a half hour and boarded a bus with only one other person on it the bus, and later got off in Tahoe City, ate pizza ($5 per slice!), and resupplied at Safeway. It’s hard to get over how expensive California is. I then headed back up into the mountains to spend the last night camping, and enjoyed a ramen, rice, and peanuts dinner next to a stream. The water was cold and tasted great. Having reached my original end point of the state park a day early, today was a nice bonus day extension.

Day 6 - 6.57 miles Today was just a few miles of hiking down into town where I caught a bus going east to Incline Village, NV. From there I bussed south to a state park then had to take an Uber a few miles. Instead of going back to the car I got out a few miles early and hiked back to reduce the Uber cost from $40 to $24. It ended up adding 2-3 hr of bland hiking. Maybe it wasnt worth it.
