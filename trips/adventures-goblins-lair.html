Adventures - Goblin's Lair

9/29/24 - Goblin's Lair

Far away from population centers in the San Rafael country lies Goblin Valley State Park. I was surprised how busy the park was that day in late September when the temperatures were in the 90s. Feeling wiped out from the three previous days of hiking and possibly the heat, I opted for a shorter hike to a landmark called “Goblin’s Lair”. I didn’t know anything about it but was pleasantly surprised when I got there after the 1.5 mile hike in. There was a short steep scramble over big boulders and down again into the cave. Inside were a couple people including one guy who was giving a tour to a mother and her daughter who apparently rappelled down from above and claimed they were exiting the cave not by climbing over the boulders, but by going in a secret tunnel below them and popping out the other side, bypassing all the scrambling. Had I brought my headlight I would have attempted it but my legs were hurting and I was tired. The “lair” was indeed pretty sick.
