Adventures - Hepworth and Roof Peaks

4/27/25 - Hepworth and Roof Peaks

Parked near the east end of the Zion tunnel and started the hike around 8:30am. Lines of cars waiting to go through the Zion tunnel had already formed, cars stretching 20 back from the tunnel entrance. I walked down the road a few hundred feet then went down into the wash. In one moment there were lines and commotion and crowds of people and then the next just silence. The first half mile was flat and sandy inside a canyon wedged between two canyon walls. I got to the exit point and climbed up the first “bowl”. It was steep and difficult to ascend because of how the rock curved to the northeast. Naturally I found myself walking northeast instead of west, as it was easier, but I had to to make frequent adjustments because there was a sheer cliff that way, and needed to go straight west. Finally after ascending the bowl, I got to a narrow staircase-like passage a few feet wide with a steep dropoff below. I continued and had to make a few difficult moves pulling myself up a few rocks. Afterwards the walking leveled out for a few minutes until another bowl came into view. I crossed through this and once again leveled out at a saddle where the views opened up tremendously. There was a 360 degree panorama view of the east Zion high country, Canaan mountains, Mt Kinesava and the high peaks to the east, which was eye candy. Up here on the saddle were clumps of ponderosa and could be a good campsite except for the lack of water. Soon there was a short descent and Hepsworth Peak and Roof Peak came into view. There was one last bowl to navigate over. After reaching the saddle I climbed up Hepsworth Peak and then back down and up to Roof Peak. Up on the summit behind the highest stone  was a small glass jar: “Trader Joe's” Corn and Chili salsa. Inside was a summit register. Since the earliest recording in late 2023 there were about 7 or 8 other names. I flipped through and wrote my name down. The way back was fast. I put the camera in the bag and high tailed it back to the steep section from earlier. I took a different way but shuffled fast over the slickrock, making it back to the car from the summits in no more than an hour. The route to the summits had taken me a leisurely 3 hours. Back was under an hour. I like to think of today's route as the “3 Bowls Trail”. It would have been nice to extend the hike but I was feeling a bit winded from the past 2-3 days of hiking and backpacking. Many things to see in this part and 0 people on the trail today. Would like to come back again. Update: Looking through the Alltrails app one night I was surprised to see this hike on there. There were maybe 2-3 reviews in the past year, and the most recent one made the hike seem like a near death experience. Internet reviews should be taken with a grain of salt.
