Adventures - Death Canyon Trail

8/22/2024 - Death Canyon Trail

On my first day in Grand Teton National Park i did an overnight backpack to Death Canyon. Looking back it isnt the most scenic hike in the park, but it was still good. The hike started out near a lake, and made a fast ascent alongside a stream with waterfalls and rushing water. After topping out the hike was flat from there through the canyon. The mountains on either side seemed to go straight up from the ground and I had to bend my head back completely to look at them. There were very few people but there were a couple campers nearby me that night, but we were far enough away from each other that I didnt hear them. Along the way there was a big moose. After setting up camp I hiked out of the canyon to the Alaska Basin crest area where the views opened up a bit more. Afterwards, I hiked the .75 miles or so back to camp, collected water from the stream near the campsite and enjoyed the cool crisp tasting water. The hike back the next morning was rather uneventful aside from another moose sighting, but it was probably the same one as the day before.
