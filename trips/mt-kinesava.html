Mt Kinesava

5/17/25 - Mt Kinesava

A steep hike up one of the peaks just outside the main entrance to Zion with a panel of petroglyphs just below the summit. I heard about this route a few months ago in Tucson when Dad mentioned something about a helicopter search and rescue in Zion. Reading the story, one or two people had attempted this route and fell on a ledge somewhere and couldn’t get back (up?) so they called search and rescue and a helicopter came in and got them out. The hike up was steep but not too difficult and marked decently with cairns. After heading straight north towards the mountain and getting up to the face of the wall, I turned and followed a narrow and steep ridgeline east. It was a very fun scramble up over boulders with a few Class 4 moves. Just below the top was the crux with some large boulders that I had to use hands and feet to pull myself over. After gaining the plateau there was a nice flat area with white sand, ponderosa, and hoodoos. Ahead was the real summit, Mt Kinesava, which wasn’t that visible from the approach or view from the start. The climb up to the top was possibly even more steep, maybe because I did my own route and went around to the backside and climbed up. There wasn’t much margin for error aside from a few feet separating myself from the straight shot down several thousand feet. But the view was fantastic and quite a rush. I looked north and could see the Bishropics and The Hamster, where I had backpacked in March. At the top I gazed down several hundred feet to the main summit seen from below the mountain. To the northeast I couldn’t help but eye the West Temple and a peak in between. There is a traverse people have done from Mt Kinesava across to the West Temple but the challenge is with the peak in the middle. It is incredible narrow, requires roping and webbing, and is basically a ridge just a few feet wide with a drop on both sides. Even from this far I could see the difficulty in getting past the middle peak but is something to bookmark for later. It looks fun. Once on top the winds picked up and were gusting pretty strong so I headed down a different way. There were a couple tricky spots and at one point my right foot slipped so I grazed my leg against a rock and slid a few feet. Just so happens that the scab I had from a few weeks ago that was almost healed got completely ripped off. Luckily I had some paper towel and bandaids to remedy the blood and wound. Back on the plateau again I headed over to a large wall where there was a nice panel of petroglyphs, of animals mostly, as usual. The sky started to darken and wind began picking up so I headed back, having an easier time going back knowing the way.
