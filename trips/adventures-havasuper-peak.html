Adventures - Havasuper Peak

3/8/25 - Havasuper Peak

Havasu Wilderness is an overlooked hiking area not far from Lake Havasu city and the busy I-40 interstate. The name Havasuper Peak I believe came from when it was ascended by a group of people and one said to another, "Have a super day". Regardless of the naming origin, this was a sweet hiking area with a dispersed camping area right next to it. In early February with nice temps there were only 1-2 other parties. I parked my car in a good flat spot and from there hiked into the wilderness area. I saw no one else the whole day... except for a wild burro. There are no trails in the area but there are few b ushes and cactuses which present little barrier, mak ing the hiking quite enjoyable . The way I took to Havasuper Peak started within a sandy wash, then I ascended a rocky ridgeline. I hiked along the ridge and was just below it when I realized I missed a way up. Not wanting to go back I scouted for a route and found a potential way. I ascended a steep gully with loose rocks and got to a very steep wall. It was solid rock but there was about 20ft of climbing. If I fell the drop down would not be good... the route up the wall went diagonally and got to a point where the drop could have been very dangerous as there wasnt much below it. Nevertheless, I climbed up using what I could for handholds then gained the ridge. Walking a quarter of a mile along the ridge was nice with views of Gold dome and the Needles (mountains) on one side and the colorado river on the other. Gold dome looked like a nice climb though could be a bit technical at the top. Maybe next winter. I looped around, tried to make it to the river but ran into a wall of dense bushes hundreds of feet deep preventing access, so i started the loop around the Needles back to the car. As i looped around i was just below a ridge and heard a loud, strange noise. It sounded like some sort of animal. I thought it was a mountain lion. I slowly took a few steps and heard t he sound again. I got my camera out and took another few steps upwards until I was almost at the ridgeline. Just as I was about at the top I heard some hooves going the other way. It turns out it was a wild burro. He galloped several hundred feet away then turned to stare at me and make defiant growling sounds. He kept watching me as I hiked past and didnt seem that used to humans. All in all, a good day of hiking- about 9-10 hours.

Photos: The drive up from Tucson, a double rainbow, the walk through a small canyon and then atop the ridge, looking at Havasuper Peak, then the walk along the ridgeline 1 ft off the ridge. Then the Needles, Colorado River, and a blurry shot of an angry burro who just wanted to left alone.
