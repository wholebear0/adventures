Signal Peak and Ten Ewe Mountain

2026-02-15 - Signal Peak and Ten Ewe Mountain

This was a fun outing for as short of a hike this was, but just getting to the start of the hike took some time. The road off Palm Canyon drive was decent at first then started to have dips where I unavoidably rubbed the front of the car against the ground a few times. After a mile I left the car and got on my bicycle, then headed towards the trailhead. It was about 1,200 ft elevation gain over nearly 6 miles to the trailhead. It was slow going and made even more tiring by the wash boarded road filled with loose rock. After nearly two hours I made it to the trailhead then hiked up through the scenic canyon to Signal Mountain. Gazing out at the endless peaks and ridges that extended to the horizon provided time to think about endless hiking routes and possibilities nearby. I had been wanting to come to the Kofa Wilderness for over one year and now here it did not disappoint. The view to the north east offered probably the best view but was partially obstructed by a lower rising peak nearby. I decided to head back down and then up an off-trail route to Ten Ewe Mountain where the view may be better. It was short but steep up to Ten Ewe but the views were indeed better I thought. On the way back down I passed by one person who was the only one I saw hiking. Back at the trailhead I got back on the bicycle and had a much different experience than coming up. Instead of taking nearly two hours up, it took about 15 minutes down and I pedaled for no more than 2 minutes in total over the 6 miles back. It was a great way to end the day and it would be nice to return and explore further into the Kofa Wilderness.
