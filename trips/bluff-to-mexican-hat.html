Adventures - Bluff to Mexican Hat

4/29/23 - San Juan River Trip

This past winter was one of the highest snowpack years on record in the UT and AZ area and I failed to connect the dots between that and river flow rates, not to mention being completely unfamiliar with the San Juan. I was eyeing this river trip and saw that a permit was needed but that all were taken, so I sent a short email to the park service asking for a permit and was granted one. A ranger called me and asked about my experience and I told him that I have kayaked calm rivers and lakes in the past. I asked if there were many rapids and he said a few but they would be no problem. Great, sounds good! That Friday afternoon I hightailed it out of Tucson and drove past Phoenix, Flagstaff, and into the Navajo reservation. It wasnt easy driving through the Phoeniux rush hour with the kayak inside the car blocking the right side mirror but all was well. I got up to the camp area late Friday night and found it quite full so I parked near the boat dock despite the no camping sign, hell, there was simply nowhere else to go. Another party was camping on the boat ramp itself, not sure how that worked out being sloped and all. The next morning the car shuttle service showed up and I handed over the keys. At that point my kayak was all packed with belongings inside dry bags stowed away inside the kayak. The lady asked if that was all I was taking. Everyone else nearby were taking pack rafts and busy loading crate after crate of items and crap into their raft. I set out and immediately the flow rate was high. Without paddling I went along at between 5-7mph in the chocolate brown water, but the river was calm and without rapids.
