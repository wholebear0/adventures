Cottonwood Wash

2026-04-10 - Cottonwood Wash

The first mile and a half in is an uneventful walk through a sandy wash, but the canyon quickly narrows and the sand is replaced by slick rock and boulders. Along the way are a few minor chokestone obstacles which can be passed by basic stem moves up and over them. The canyon forks and taking a right follows the main trail. A short bit later there is a substantial chokestone which is shaped like a downward facing arrow. At first glance it seems unpassable but there was a somewhat thin and antique looking rope hanging down, though I found it to be a rope to nowhere as it didn't help. The opening above was too far above and crowssing the gap to ascend through it was too wide. Perhaps a more advanced stem move could take one past. After closer inspection I noticed a different way but from below by first crouching down and going below the boulders. There was a tiny window 8 ft above just wide enough for a reasonable fit individual to fit through. There were a few rocks as footholds and I squeezed through the opening. Any adrenaline soon evaporated as just around the corner was a winding slot with a deep plunge pool filled with water. It has been a dry spring but there was even a a bathtub ring 5ft above its current level showing where the water had been. The water looked at least 6-8ft deep and was very cold. Not knowing how long it was and covered with open cuts on my legs and arms from the previous day navigating the Leprachaun slot, I persuaded myself to turn around, glancing over my shoulder back at the slot as I left. I believe this pool is known for having unpredictable depths, some people find it shallow or non existent, others find it very deep. I hope to return in the future more prepared with perhaps a small inner tube to float on and a dry bag to keep the camera and cell phone dry. Till next time!
