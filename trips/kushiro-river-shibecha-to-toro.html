Kushiro River, Shibecha to Toro

6/19/26 - Kushiro River, Shibecha to Toro

On the Nishiki river n ear Hiroshima about a week earlier a nice couple I met recommended the Kushiro river for a rafting trip. After doing more research, the upper parts of this river are known to be more chaotic with obstructions like bends, rapids, and downed trees, while the lower half is more calm, eventually working its way through the port city of Kushiro to the sea. I opted to do the lower half, from Shibecha, down 16 miles to the small town of Toro where there is a train station that one can take back to Shibecha. The put in location in Shibecha was near a bridge and the surrounding area was full of colorful wildflowers. I parked at the closest public spot I could which appeared to be a frisbee disc golf course. As I walked over to the put in, a few kids were walking to school. One group stood out, I saw two girls walking together, one on a bicycle and the other limping along. I would think the limping girl would get more use out of the bicycle . On the river the water flow was excellent with no rapids which provided the opportunity to just sit back and relax and let the flow take the raft. There was an abundance of wildlife everywhere, including birds and tons of deer along the banks and some crossing the river. The deer were very shy and would let out a high pitch shriek and run back into the forest when I got within 100 yards of them. Sounds of owls and birds echoed out onto the river from the forest on both sides. There were no fisherman and no other people until the takeout where there seemed to be some canoe outfitters launching. It was a very enjoyable trip and I am glad I was informed about it and got to do it. The takeout was muddy and followed by a half mile walk to the town of Toro with its one room, unstaffed train station. After taking the one car train from Toro to Shibecha, I ate ramen in a restaurant at a table by the window. I gazed out and saw the same two girls from the morning walking by. The same one was on the bike rolling right along without pedaling, her friend limping along next to her.
