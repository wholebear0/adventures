Tonopah NV

7/6/2025 - Tonopah NV

There is just something about those small Western towns like Tonopah, NV. Full of charm, clean, quiet, relaxed, and like a portal back to a bygone era. On the way back from the Arc Dome wilderness I stopped and walked around part of the former mining town for a bit. It was a Sunday morning around 7 am and I walked towards one shop that seemed to be open, a café. Next to the cafe was a gym with a man running on a treadmill. I entered the coffee shop and walked over white floor tiles and high ceilings. At the front by the window sat an overweight man with a teal shirt, long white beard, and camo hat, sipping his morning coffee. Attached to the café was an open door to a hotel with a very cool lobby outfitted with antique furniture and decorations. Afterwards I went outside and walked around, enjoying the cool 70 degree high elevation air (a touch over 6,000’) that had not a drop of humidity. I drove out of town taking note of a sign mentioning the next gas station was in 162 miles. I drove fast across the high desert, eventually passing the small community with said gas station, and a VFW post, known as Rachel, NV, then reached the next town, Caliente, NV. It was 192 miles from Tonopah and another quaint little town almost as cool as Tonopah, but I didnt stop to take any photos.
