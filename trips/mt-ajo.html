Mt Ajo

2025-12-26 - Mt Ajo

This was a nice peaceful hike. To get there one must turn off route 85 onto Ajo Mountain Drive and drive the slightly wash boarded gravel road to the far end of the one way loop, about 12 miles. From the trailhead, I think most people hike a small loop up to the Bull Pasture Loop and don't go up to the top. Aside from 2 groups of people around the viewpoint area which is about a mile and a half in, there was no one else the whole hike. The climb is relatively short but steep in spots then levels out for a mile or so along a ridge with nice desert views in all directions. Looking east over the vast expanse of the Tohono O’Odham reservation I could spot the Catalina Mountains and Baboquivari Peak, and to the south the town of Sonoyta, Mexico. The top ridge had many teal agaves and some small juniper trees and brush. The rock was solid and tended to have small alcoves or cavities carved out in spots which made it even more fun.
