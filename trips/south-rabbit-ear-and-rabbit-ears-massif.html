South Rabbit Ear and Rabbit Ears Massif

11/2/2025 - South Rabbit Ear and Rabbit Ears Massif

Just to the east of Las Cruces, NM is a small but unusual mountain range known as the Organ Mountains. Filled with pinnacles in which it is named after, the range has quite a few nice hikes that I would like to come back to and explore when the opportunity comes up in the future. This hike started from the side of the road, followed along a mile and a half of abandoned jeep road to an old mine, then was an off-trail rock scramble 2,500 feet up to the Rabbit Ears plateau. From there was a brief summit south to the Rabbit Ears Massif and north to South Ear pinnacle. The off trail nature made this relatively short 7 mile hike take about 7 hours, nearly twice as long as if there had been an established trail. It was a fun hike with not another person around and had complete solitude.

Sun coming up over the Organ Mountains around 7:30 am.

Old mining camp house. The mine is a half mile or so further up the trail.

The old mine with open entrances which is unusual. Near the opening it smelled like some sort of oil, still present after probably over one hundred years.

The official trail ended at the mine but a social trail continued, wrapping around the hill and leading up through a canyon. From there the hiking was mostly through rocks and boulders all the way up to the Rabbit Ear plateau, a grueling couple of miles.

I tried to bypass some of the rock hopping by taking a hill but that proved to be fruitless as the vegetation was thick and terrain steep.

At the plateau, looking north at South Rabbit Ear. I wasnt sure what the best route to climb that would be so I hiked up the Rabbit Ear Massif to the south to get a better view.
