Ventana Canyon and Window Peak

11/23/2025 - Ventana Canyon and Window Peak

The hike up through Ventana Canyon to the Window (rock formation) and Window Peak requires a bit of work, about 4,500 ft of elevation gain. But it is on a clearly marked trail so it’s not bad though slightly overgrown on the upper portions. Near Window Peak there are tons of really cool rock formations like pinnacles, windows between rocks, spires, and formations looking like fingers, and even a towering person looking out over the canyon. As with other hikes in the area, this one starts at city level surrounded by Saguaros then climbs through different ecosystems like forests and riparian, and eventually up to pine trees. This was a really cool hike made even better by clouds and flowing water from the previous three days of rain. The sound of flowing water was pretty much everywhere along the trail cascading hundreds of feet down rocky slopes or down along the creek. The views are nice throughout the trail but get even better once atop the ridge at the junction with the Esperero trail. From there affords a view west of Oro Valley and east towards the cragly spires near Window Peak. I had been lucky the few hours up to now as the clouds seem ot have lifted as I hiked, but intensified as I hiked up from the ridge. The trail wrapped around the base of Window Peak so I went off trail and followed a cairned route up to the summit, eventually wedging myself between a small tree and rock to push myself up to the actual summit. At this point I was mostly clouded out with limited visibility but could still make out great views in all directions. I was about 5 hours in and though wishing to stay much longer to explore the area, headed back down, eventually hiking through an hour of complete darkness. Afterwards, in looking at the map, there could be a nice loop route by taking the Ventana Trail up to Window Peak and down the Esperero Trail into Sabino Canyon though this would require a car shuttle to get back to the Ventana Trailhead.
