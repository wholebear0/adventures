Cerbat Pinnacles via Antelope Canyon

11/7/2025 - Cerbat Pinnacles via Antelope Canyon

The Cerbat pinnacles are outcroppings in the Mt. Tipton wilderness by the town of Dolan Springs, AZ. I drove through a spread out residential area with increasingly rough roads, sometimes turning around due to the roads being in such bad shape. Making matters even worse is a disconnect in road naming. The BLM website says to take Ocotillo Drive then Orchid Street, then Palm Dr. There is Ocotillo Road and Orchid Drive, but Palm Street is pretty much unnavigable unless one has a high clearance (lifted) 4x4, so I took an unnamed but still quite bumpy road. I had to park about a mile short from the actual trailhead as it wouldn’t be passable to anything less than a lifted 4x4 due to increasingly off camber sections. The hike was a bushwhack through thick cactus like Cholla and went up Antelope Canyon. From atop the initial saddle any remnants of a trail entirely disappeared so I continued southeast before turning to head up a canyon below the pinnacles. It was slow going through dense bushes and vegetation and rock quality was low in the ascent towards the pinnacles. After finally gaining the ridge the pinnacles were unclimbable due to exposure and questionable rock quality, therefore views to the other side were limited. The views to the east were decent and reminded me of Big Bend National Park. Overall, the hike was like the drive up. Frustrating, slow going, and unremarkable. Count on withstanding multiple cholla stabbings. This hike will go in the one and done bucket.
