Prisoners Harbor to Scorpion via Montanon Trail

6/6/2025 - Prisoners Harbor to Scorpion via Montanon Trail Wanting a taste of some different scenery, I took the day off Friday and drove to Ventura, CA the Thursday before and slept in the car at the ferry company parking lot. The next morning I took the 9 am ferry to Santa Cruz island which was about an hour and a half. Midway across a whale surfaced though only its back and fin could be seen. The ferry first stopped at the main anchorage with the visitor center and campground where 95% of people got off. The island used to be a private ranch and therefore has a few old, unused roads, but is otherwise completely undeveloped. There is no food and water can only be found at the Scorpion campground. After the passengers were dropped off we went to the last stop, Prisoner’s Harbor. From here it was a bout a 14 mile hike back to the Scorpion campground area where I would camp and refill water. The hike was really pleasant and peaceful and I didn’t see a single other person until arriving at the campground around 6:30 pm. After leaving the dock, the trail climbed up along a ridge and weaved through lush hills dotted with wildflowers, succulents, and other unique plants. For a few miles the trail continued along a crest up about 1,300 ft with a view of the ocean below on both sides. Then the trail climbed up a ridgeline where the weather was quite foggy. After hanging out on the ridge for a few minutes and taking in the views, I went over it and the weather finally cleared up enough for the sun to poke through ever so slightly. The gradual descent down the hills to the campsite was nice. There were a few other groups at the campsite and round dusk several foxes appeared from the tall grass outside the campground and wandered through camp, probably in search of food.
