Castle Dome

2/14/2026 - Castle Dome

Castle Dome is a box shaped peak in the Kofa Wilderness that seems to be visible from anywhere in the vicinity, whether elsewhere in the wilderness or 35 miles to the south near Yuma, AZ. The road in off highway 95 is doable in any car but there is a turnoff onto a much less good road. It was from there that I transitioned from car to bicycle to get to the trailhead. It took about an hour of mountain biking up a gradual climb of 600 ft or so. There were no cars except for one or two groups of offroad vehicles that passed by and waved. Finally making it to the trailhead, the going from there was through a wash and then a canyon which eventually widened out. I saw a desert toad and a foot long rattlesnake. The way up to Castle Dome was well marked and over scree and uneven footing most of the way. At one point there was a tall pinnacle and a 30 foot vertical wall next to it. It seemed like the only way up and there were some hand/footholds, but there definitely was some exposure. On the way down I found a much easier and somewhat hidden way so I piled a few rocks together into an arrow hoping others would see it. From there on the views were phenomenal in all directions. There was a climb of maybe 200 ft to the summit and on top there was a nice assortment of low desert plants like cholla, ocotillo, agave, palo verde, and red barrel cactus. There wasn’t a single other person out but in the summit register I did see a few entries from the past month or two.
