Adventures - Lower Death Canyon Slot to Wolverine Canyon Loop

5/24/2025 - Lower Death Canyon Slot to Wolverine Canyon Loop

This loop trail was about an hour and a half from the nearest town, Boulder, UT. But the road from the Boulder area was absolutely breathtaking. It twisted and turned, and climbed on top of mesas, then down through cottonwood lined canyons. According to an information sign overlooking town, Boulder was the last town to rely on mule driven mail and the first road out of town was f inally built in the 1940s by the CCC. Most of the hour and a half drive from Boulder was on a pretty decent dirt road and was very remote. There were a couple cars along the way, probably hikers. I got in a little after 7pm and drove past the trailhead to a spot and camped. The hike was no less spectacular. It started off in a wide canyon with juniper and dwarf ponderosa surrounded by red sandstone walls. Gradually the canyon narrowed and after 3 miles the narrows started. 2 miles of narrows gave way to a slot. For at least three miles there was shoulder width or slightly wider slot to walk through. Turn after turn of the canyon presented new views of the slot and seemingly never ended. After a couple miles weaving through the gorgeous slot came the first water obstacle. There was a drop of a few feet into a chocolate brown body of water of unknown depth. I estimated it to be hip level. After contemplation I decided to step up and over the narrows onto a slick rock bench which proved to be a good choice but after walking a hundred feet or so I realized there didn't seem to be a way back down. Fortunately, just then the only party I had yet to see came into view hiking towards me. They had camped overnight by the river. One of the guys pointed out a way down was worse than it looked , I just had to leap across the slot then shimmy down. I was thankful and his group continued up and through the water. He mentioned there was one other person behind him that also didn't want to get his feet wet and after walking a few hundred feet we ran into each other, except he was about 10 feet off the ground. As I turned a corner I saw the kid. He looked about 18 years old and was solo hiking and was up perched at least 10 ft above the water pool attempting to stem his way across. I had no idea how he was standing where he was as the rock was nearly vertical. He hesitated and said I could pass but I was afraid he was going to fall and snap a limb in half. I estimated a 50/50 chance of that happening so I hung around even after he said I could pass if desired. I hung around and offered to help so he took off his backpack and tossed it to me. It was about 20lb and nearly knocked me over. Ultimately, he ended up finessing his way down which was rather impressive. As much as I don't like getting my feet wet, this kid took it to the next level. He really didn't want to get his feet wet. Ultimately, I put on my wading shoes and went through the brown water, then dried my feet off and put the shoes back on. It was a bit premature as there was another big pool ahead. It looked deep, perhaps chest deep and there was no way around this one. I used my hiking pole to probe the depth and watched as the entire pole went straight down, then I picked it back up. After a few minutes of deliberation, I found a small whole below the boulder. I took off my shoes and tied them to my backpack, then took the backpack off as I wouldnt fit wearing it. I squeezed through the hole while contorting myself and descended 4 feet, then pulled the backpack down. The water was indeed about waist deep or a little below that, but it wasn't bad. That ended up being the last pool and I put my shoes back on for the rest of the hike. That was a blast. I reached the mouth of the canyon and junction then took wolverine canyon back up. It was about 9 more miles back to the car. Wolverine canyon was nice though death hollow was better. I ran into one group of people headed down canyon to spend the night. Shortly before reaching the car there was a bunch of petrified wood which was awesome. Some of the wood was just like a tree, it fell over long ago, broke into pieces and was petrified on the spot. Petrified wood forms when wood is submerged for long periods of time in water and is replaced by minerals, changing it into stone.
