Adventures - Slickhorn Canyon

4/17/2026 - Slickhorn Canyon

Slickhorn canyon is a fairly big canyon with several forks, all of which converge at some point and drain into the San Juan river perhaps 10 miles to the south. The canyon is known for having dwellings and petroglyphs throughout. I chose to hike the main fork, the first fork, down to the confluence with the second fork. The road in along Point Lookout Road was somewhat rocky and washboarded and you need to turn off to the trailhead. The road from here is only a quarter of a mile but very rough and off camber, and is unsuitable for a sedan. The hike was pleasant, first starting at the top of the mesa then working down into the canyon. There is no official trail but a social trail that leads down to an impassable pour off, then around it buy walking along the rim, eventually making a steep descent into the canyon. From there one just needs to follow the sandy wash. A landmark called the Perfect Kiva is less than a mile from getting to the canyon bottom and is up near the rim below an alcove.
