Mount Agassiz and Naturalist Basin

7/13/2025 - Mount Agassiz and Naturalist Basin

Really enjoyable loop hike combining high elevation off trail ridgeline and then on-trail parts near the lower elevation basin lakes. This hike took nearly 10 hours because of slow going through steep boulder and talus areas. I started the hike at 8:00 a.m. and there were two other people, a man and his daughter. They invited me to hike together with them so I accepted and just followed for the first mile since they knew the way to the top of the ridge. He said he just retired and lives in Park City and is big into skiing and his daughter had just graduated college and was about to embark on a six-week backpacking trip through Vietnam. They were nice. Towards the top we ran into one girl who seemed a little lost so the guy invited her to hike with us. The girl said she used to work at Cotopaxi but is now fun-employed. On top of the ridge the guy and his daughter headed left toward Mount Hayden and the girl sat to enjoy the view and presumably headed back down afterwards. I headed to the right towards Mount Agassiz. It was a long mile and a half scramble across boulders and loose rock but the views were fantastic: the glacier carved cirque surrounded by mountains, and basin and lakes to the east. During the traverse there were a few notches which required descending down a hundred feet or so and then coming back up to bypass them. On the last obstacle before the hike up to Mt Agassiz I skirted around a pinnacle and ended up at a little saddle point below the peak. From there it was a several hundred feet climb up and was tiring, but it looked worse than it was. The views at the top at 12,428' were fantastic. To make it a loop hike I decided to descend a different way by going down towards naturalist basin and saw only one feasible way down. I figured going down would be like the way up but I was wrong. They were tons of small rocks and dirt and pretty much every step my feet would slip out and trigger a mini rockslide. A few times my feet slid out and I fell on my backside since I walked leaning into the slope. Rocks would tumble down the steep slopes and I watched, partially amused but also a little nervous. Lesson learned that side hilling through scree on a steep slope is not only not fun but dangerous. It was a risky route and even though I went very slow by taking a full hour to go a quarter of a mile or less, I wouldn’t recommend taking this way down. Once down at lake level the mosquitoes were relentless and I instantly regretted not applying bug spray. There were a few lakes that were very clear and had trout jumping up out of the water. The surrounding country was pine trees and streams and was very picturesque.  The five miles back to the car went through nice pine forest and were relatively flat and uneventful but still pleasant.
