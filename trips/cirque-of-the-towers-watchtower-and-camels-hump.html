Adventures - Cirque of the Towers, Watchtower, and Camels Hump

8/1/2025 - 8/3/2025 - Cirque of the Towers, Watchtower, and Camels Hump

After maybe 40 miles of dirt road, the last 15 miles of it quite brutal with wash boarded and deeply rutted sections, I made it to the popular Big Sandy Trailhead. It was about 3 pm and the weather was a bit ominous with rain clouds and wind gusting. But after starting the hike and walking for an hour or so the wind blew everything out and revealed a nice blue sky. I hiked along the Continental Divide Trail for over 5 miles then turned right to a trail leading to Donald Lake. Despite the crowded parking lot I had only seen one or two other groups. I got to Donald Lake and had it pretty much to myself except for one group camping near the beginning of the lake. I found a nice spot, spent a cold night wearing four layers of clothes, then headed off trail towards Elizabeth couloir which I would climb and use to access Watchtower Peak. It was a bush whack through a mile or so of dense forest and a climb up to the col but the views up there were worth it. After summiting Watchtower peak I planned to climb down the other side of the col to Shadow Lake directly below but couldn’t find a way. There were only sheer drops and nasty looking gullies. With a possible backtrack in mind, I climbed up to Watchtower peak and viewed into the other side of the Cirque of the Towers. There were many pinnacles and cool upside down cone looking formations along the way. I wanted to go to another peak, Pylon Peak, but decided against it as the views would be pretty much the same and because I knew I may have to backtrack which would add an additional 6 or 7 miles to the day. I hiked down and returned to the col, and after giving up on the idea of taking the direct but extremely dangerous way down to Shadow Lake, I bushwhacked back to Donald Lake, reconnected with the Continental Divide trail for a few miles, then turned off on the Shadow Lake trail. By the time I got to the lake the weather shifted to clouds and was somewhat chilly, but the weather was still pleasant. I found a nice flat area and set up camp. From the other side of the col at lake level I couldn’t see a way down from this angle either. The next morning  I continued hiking through the basin passing Billy’s Lake, Barren Lake, and Texas Lake, then climbed Texas Pass which was a bunch of scree and loose dirt. From the pass I took an off trail jaunt up to Camel’s Hump peak which was really fun. There were a few spots with fun scrambling and just one spot with some stemming between boulders. From atop I could see the lakes I had passed not long before, and 360 degree view in all directions. The route down to the popular Lonesome Lake was nice and had great views of the Cirque: Pingora Peak, Watchtower Peak I had climbed the day before, Warrior Peaks, and many others. After hiking more I hiked through Jackass Pass the down across Arrowhead Lake  and Big Sandy Lake. From Big Sandy Hike it was about a 5 mile hike through forest back to the car along a gradually descending trail. All in all, despite the route audible, it was a nice hike though I wish I could have spent longer, maybe another 3-4 days going deeper into the mountains and off trail exploring the innumerable peaks.

After backtracking down to Donald Lake, I reconnected with the Continental Divide Trail and turned right onto the Shadow Lake trail, making a big loop around the impassable route.

Shadow Lake. Elizabeth Col towards the middle right

Elizabeth col from my campsite near Shadow Lake. From below I still don’t see a way down. When on top the only route I saw was the one in red, a steep direct way down which I ruled out due to risk. The one resource I had mentioned a ledge system near Elizabeth peak just off the col but I didnt see a viable way. I could see a possible route, in purple, that has one going about halfway up the peak then cutting down which looks doable, but from atop I remember that slope being loose dirt and likely too narrow to walk, with serious exposure. Otherwise maybe the route in blue looks like the safest way. It would require one to go up and over Elizabeth peak, then making a drop straight down, probably being the crux of the route. After that it looks like smooth sailing. There was no way to see this route from on top. Regardless, I think the safest way to get to Shadow Lake is to go back around even if that means adding the 4-5 hours of hiking it took.
