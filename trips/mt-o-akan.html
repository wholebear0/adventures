Mt O-Akan

6/18/26 - Mt O-Akan

Next to the Ainu onsen town of Lake Akan in the Akan-Mashū National Park, is Mt O-Akan that looks out over the surrounding countryside. The start of this hike is really nice and follows along a flowing stream which empties into a couple small lakes. The color of the water, clarity, and gre enery were eye candy. After a half mile the ascent starts and it's just forest hiking up to near the top where the trees get much shorter and are more bush like . It was a st eep trail, gaining over 1,000 ft per mile, for 3 miles. The last 3/4 of a mile the trail is very narrow and requires one to basically push through branches and brush. Fortunately there are no thorns and the vegetation is soft and easy to push past but annoying nevertheless. At the peak there are nice 360 degree views. On the other side of the lake in the distance is Mt Me-Akan which I tried to hike two days ago but had to turn around due to trail closure from volcanic activity risk. Later that day at a natural, free onsen right on Lake Kussharo 45 minutes away, a guy visiting from Tokyo t o l d me that this region, the K ushiro region, is very special in Japan.
