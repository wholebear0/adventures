Klothos Temple

2026-02-16 - Klothos Temple

Not sure how Klothos Temple got its name but it was a fun hike and the journey to a the top had great views of the area. I didn’t encounter anyone else the whole time though did see 5 or 6 names in the summit register going back to last winter. The hike started out from a trailhead where there were a handful of other cards though I believe people were hiking a popular loop in a different area of the wilderness. The hike started through a wash and immediately offered views of Muggins Mountain in the distance. Shortly afterwards I jumped off trail and pressed on to Klothos Temple, encountering a few cairns along the way. It was fairly easy going and well marked until a scree field which was a bit painful on the already sore ankles. The scree slope was probably the most difficult and least fun portion of the hike. After that the route continued along ridgeline until the peak, had great views, and was very fun. Klothos Temple itself was hidden from view behind other lower peaks for most of the hike. The route wrapped around various ridgelines coming from these peaks but finally Klothos Temple came into view. On the summit there were some aromas of fertilizer or manure which had possibly wafted over from the farms and agricultural fields to the west.
