Adventures - Coral Pink Sand Dunes State Park

4/20/2024 - Coral Pink Sand Dunes State Park

For a month or two I had been eyeing maps trying to find an accessible way into the Moquith Mountain Wilderness Area. I couldnt find much if any information on this area which peaked my interest. From what I gathered the only way in would mean a dozen or so miles of rough, unmaintained sandy roads which my car definitely couldnt handle, and that would be too long for a day hike. Scrutinizing the map more revealed a possible alternative, starting just outside Coral Pink Sand Dunes state park and hiking a in a few miles on unknown road to what looked like an access point to get down into the canyon. When I got to the turn off there were numerous jeeps and off road vehicles and the road was too sandy to continue driving. Not surprised, I parked the car and hiked in about 1.75 miles passing one or two vehicles along the way. I turned left off the road and made my way down into the canyon. There wasnt no trail and the going was slow. After a half mile I got to a ledge with a 50 foot or so dropoff which was unpassable. With no way around it turned around and walked back to the car in disappointment. The canyon walls are just too steep in this area to go down but maybe another side canyon could offer a route but I havent been back to explore.

After hiking back to the car and driving to the main area of the park
