Submarine Rock, Sawtooth Highpoint, and Pinhole Peak

2026-01-03 - Submarine Rock, Sawtooth Highpoint, and Pinhole Peak

West of Picacho Peak is one section of the Ironwood Forest National Monument known as the Sawtooth Mountains. It contains several nice peaks including Submarine Rock aka Little Picacho, Pinhole Peak, and the Sawtooth Mountains High Point. I had been wanting to come out for a couple years but was hesitant as this area had been a known drug/migrant smuggling route. After some trip reports this year describing the area as “tamed” I ventured out and brought my mountain bike because of not knowing much about the 5 miles of dirt road to get near the peaks. Hauling the bike proved to be a wise decision as the road was quite sandy and my suv may have gotten stuck. After getting close to Submarine Rock I unfasted the hiking poles which I bungee corded to the bike frame and headed up the slope. Along the base of the peak was a discarded Fila shoe and an oddly placed backpack with a hole in the center. I stopped for a moment and looked though the hole and thought there was something reflective in there but decided not to investigate further. About 20 minutes of some scree climbing later I made it to the peak which was narrow but pretty easy and not as dangerous as it looks. At the top I heard a helicopter come by which made a giant circle around the little mountain range I was in. Later my thoughts returned to the discarded but clean and neatly placed backpack, wondering if perhaps there was a trip sensor in it. The timing of the helicopter would have lined up. After Submarine Rock I descended and made my way over to the high point, passing more discarded clothing in washes along the way. Near the high point summit was a cool arch and one that looks to have been a smuggling hideout due to the amount of trash nearby- old cans, beer cans, food and other items just littered without care. What a shame. After the arch and on top of the high point were some more nice views albeit with more trash like sweaters, black and bottles. There were a few more shoes too, like more Fila’s, which by pattern seems to have been a favorite sneaker type among smugglers. Nearly all the trash looked like it had been there for some time, from a different era. After hiking back to the bicycle, I biked over to Pinhole Peak and made the quick ascent, making an easy Class 4 move to go up and out of the pinhole.
