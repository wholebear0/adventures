Adventures - Padre Island North Beach and USS Lexington

10/19/2025 - Padre Island North Beach and USS Lexington

It had been 5 years since the last visit to Padre Island so I packed the car, drove on the beach for about a mile then camped overnight. It was very windy and humid, leaving a layer of salt on the car. The water was quite warm as I remember it being in November last time I was there. But there were no crowds, few people, and just overall a nice experience to stake out a piece of shoreline several feet from the water and get some rest and relaxation. The freedom of a drivable beach that is free to camp with no permits needed is hard to beat. In Corpus I stopped to see the USS Lexington, a WW2 aircraft carrier that saw a lot of use and action in the WW2 pacific theater, most notably the battle at Tarawa. It has been credited with shooting down over 380 aircraft and also withstood a kamikaze attack which crashed into the upper tower area killing about 30-40 people. The ship was repaired afterwards and today there is the military flag of Japan marking the exact spot where the kamikaze hit, which you can see in the photos. Most of the carrier has been converted into a museum and it was fascinating to navigate up and down narrow stairwells, going from room to room. There was the bed area (they call it the "berthing" area, enging room, dining room, dental clinic, hospital, surgery room, a room for church services, and others. There was so much information and exhibits that time seemed to melt away. I tried to see as much as I could but had to tell myself to leave so there would be enough time at the beach. Very cool museum.
