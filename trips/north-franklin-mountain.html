North Franklin Mountain

11/1/2025 - North Franklin Mountain

Just outside of El Paso cutting the city into two halves is a minor mountain range called the Franklin Mountains. I drove in from San Antonio earlier in the day and found out that the park closes its gates at 5 pm. I started the hike around 1:30 pm and had the trail run in spots to make sure I got back to the car with enough time to exit the park before the gates closed. The park ranger said anyone closed in would have to camp at the campground, which wouldn't have been a big issue. It was a nice hike up to an old Cave once inhabited by Native Americans many years ago. Then the trail ascended up to a saddle overlooking the far northeastern suburbs of El Paso, then at the summit overlooked South Franklin Mountains and near the central part of El Paso and Ciudad Juarez just past in the distance. Ultimately, I made it back to the car with time to spare though wishing there was another day or two to explore more of the peaks the park offered... Next time I suppose. Afterwards I grabbed some tacos a couple miles away at Taco Tote, then drove north in the setting west Texas sun, towards the Organ Mountains in New Mexico to spend the night.

Aztec cave

The cave was inhabited by Native Americans many years ago and the ceilings were black, evidence of cooking and inhabitance many years ago.

The official trail ended at the cave but I took a social trail that wrapped around and went above the cave.
