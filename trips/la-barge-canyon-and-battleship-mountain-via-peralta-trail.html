La Barge Canyon and Battleship Mountain via Peralta Trail

3/13/2026 - 3/14/2026 - La Barge Canyon and Battleship Mountain via Peralta Trail

This loop hike started from the Peralta Trailhead which is one of the more popular access points into the Superstition Mountains just east of Phoenix. The road in was gravel and slightly washboarded though passable by any car. Starting the hike at 8 am it was already hot and without shade. The first half of the trail was decent and had some okay views of mountains in the distance. After turning off onto the Cavalry trail the scenery deteriorated fast. There were burned trees in what looked like a fire fro ma few years back. Vegetation had started to regrow but the impact was still visible. The  trail got difficult to follow and meandered over the mostly stagnant La Barge creek for a mile or two. Late in the afternoon I found myself very thirsty and in desire of water. The creek at this point was nasty and full of algae so I held off until something better. A short while later a canyon with steep walls formed around the creek so I went off trail and down into the canyon. The creek at this point was flowing ever so slowly and still had many flies and some algae but I refilled here anyway, knowing that conditions wouldnt improve much ahead. After nearly 3 hours bushwhacking and boulder hopping, I finished the mile or so of the canyon and came out at La Barge Pools. With steep near vertical walls, this and Battleship Mountain were the highlights and most enjoyable parts of the hike. That night I camped at a saddle below Battleship Mountain and set out to climb it early the next morning. It was a fun scramble with some exposure in spots but there was always a class 3 or below way of moving forward. From atop Battleship Mountain one can see Canyon Lake to the north. The hike back to the car followed along the boulder trail which was a drag. It was two hours of stone stepping. Like the day before, it was hot and I started counting down the miles to the end. The last phase of the hike, Peralta Canyon, was nice and could be a good day hike but being at the end of the loop and me being very thirsty and almost out of water I didnt enjoy it much. The area may be best hiked in winter months like Nov-Feb when high temps are below 80.
