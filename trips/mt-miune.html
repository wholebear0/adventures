Mt Miune

6/8/26 - Mt Miune

After turning off the highway near the coast, it took over 2 hours to traverse slick, winding roads through gorges and up into the mountains to the Mt Tsurugi area. Most of the road was a single lane so it had to be shared with cars going the other direction. Complicating matters was how the road was, going up along narrow gorges meant many turns. Each turn, and there were hundreds, required slowing down and looking into a mirror affixed at the end of a pole. Even small villages had a single land and curving roads so a few times I nearly drove into vehicles and had to backup into someone's parking area to let the other pass. It was stressful but fun white knuckle driving that kept me on my toes. I liked it. The Mt Tsurugi area is supposed to be nice, an area that samurai had once inhabited after losing a battle and fleeing into the mountains. They were known to raise and ride horses through the mountaintops. I also heard that nearby Mt Miune was nice as well, so I planned a route to see both, first starting west of Mt Tsurugi a few miles, climbing to Mt Miune, then hiking the ridgeline over to Mt Tsurugi, then road walking or hitchhiking back to where I parked. Ultimately, I made it only to Mt Miune and a few surrounding peaks and not Mt Tsurugi due to heavy rainfall and fog. The hike up from Mt Miune trailhead was almost as miserable as it gets. Incredibly damp practically 100% humidity, clouded out with no visibility, topped with no trail at all for 2 miles or so was no fun. I got to an area with what appeared to be a bear cage, then from there no trail existed. I ascended probably 2000 ft on steep muddy and leafy slopes, sidehilling and postholing most of the time trying to find any way up. At times I had to grab hold of trees to move forward since the ground was so wet and steep. It took two hours but muddy and wet I eventually reconnected with an actual trail and later the shelter hut on Mt Miune. My clothes and shoes were soaked and there was no one else in the hut so I hung everything up to dry. I'm not surprised I hadnt run into anyone yet given the conditions, maybe also because it was a Monday. Hoping the weather improves the next day I was disapppointed to find it not much better in the morning. The night was extremely windy and rainy and the next morning was still cloudy. I waited and some of it cleared up so I immediately set out. It was clear for an hour, just long enough to make it to the top of Mt Miune to snap some photos. The plan was to continue to Mt Tsurugi another 7 miles but it started raining and didn't stop. Combined with 20 mph wind, cold, complete dampness, wet shoes, and poor visibility, I cut the hike short and descended early. It was a rough hike. The way down was actually pretty nice. The trail followed along one stream at first then several others joined it. Near the mountain base all the streams connected to form a rushing creek with many other streams coming from the mountains and feeding into it. Just before the road was a vine bridge which was neat.

Wandering around. No trail at all.
